## 6. STICKINESS

Stickiness varies significantly by format. Industrial tenants with specialized equipment installations are the stickiest. Retail tenants with branded fit-outs have moderate stickiness. Office tenants with standard layouts are the least sticky.

Relocation difficulty is highest where:
- Tenant has invested in specialized infrastructure
- The format is operationally integrated (not just aesthetics)
- Regulatory approvals are tied to the specific address

## 8. STRESS TESTS

**AI/tech displacement risk:** Medium. Back-office functions are increasingly automated. Physical formats tied to in-person service or logistics are more resilient.

**Pandemic or emergency shock:** Moderate risk for retail; lower risk for industrial. During COVID-19, essential-format tenants held; discretionary retail collapsed.

**Regulatory risk:** Low to medium. No specific regulatory concentration risk identified in CA/DR for this sector.

**Oversupply risk:** Medium in Panama and Costa Rica, where retail construction has outpaced demand in certain corridors.

**Disintermediation risk:** Low for physical-format tenants. E-commerce displacement risk is real for some retail sub-sectors but has not materialized at scale in CA/DR.

**FX / U.S.-cycle exposure:** Medium. Several CA/DR economies are highly dollarized or USD-tied. U.S. recession would reduce remittances and consumer spending.

## 11. RECOMMENDATION

**Acceptable countries:** Panama, Costa Rica (primary); Guatemala, Dominican Republic (selective); El Salvador (watchlist)
