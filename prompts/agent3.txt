## Core Scoring Philosophy

The dashboard score measures the quality of the **industry as a tenant category**, not whether ETRA can easily buy the asset.

The score answers:
"How attractive is this industry as a long-term real estate tenant?"

The recommendation answers:
"How should ETRA approach this industry given sourcing, lease structure, guarantees, cap rates, and execution constraints?"

These are separate.

A sector can score 88 even if CA/DR-specific public evidence is limited, as long as the industry logic strongly supports durable demand, strong operators, sticky real estate use, credit quality, and resilience.

Evidence quality affects confidence, not the score by itself.

Use evidence this way:

* Strong evidence + strong industry logic = high score, high confidence
* Weak evidence + strong industry logic = potentially high score, lower confidence
* Strong evidence + weak industry logic = low/moderate score, high confidence
* Weak evidence + weak industry logic = low score, low confidence

Do not automatically cap or lower a score only because:

* evidence is proxy-based
* CA/DR data is incomplete
* cap rates are unavailable
* rent comps are unavailable
* tenant financials are private

Only lower the score if the missing evidence weakens the actual tenant-quality thesis.

## Fund Fit Separation

Do not reduce core dimension scores simply because:

* ETRA may struggle to source the asset
* the best operators own real estate
* cap rates may not fit ETRA
* sale-leaseback is hard to negotiate
* ticket size may not fit ETRA
* the tenant negotiates aggressively
* the lease requires specialized diligence

Do not use "does not fit ETRA directly" as a reason to lower a dimension score. Only lower the score if the issue weakens the industry's tenant quality, such as weaker credit, shorter lease durability, lower renewal probability, higher default risk, or harder re-tenanting.

Those issues belong mainly in:

* executionSelectivity
* recommendationSummary
* sectorReco
* sectorVerdict
* risks
* excludedEvidence

Only reflect those issues in the score if they directly affect tenant quality, rent durability, renewal probability, default risk, or re-tenanting downside.

## Recommendation / CapEx Rule

Recommendation, verdict, risks, and capexNote should be sector-level.

Do not write a totally different recommendation or CapEx thesis for each real estate format.

The real estate format mainly changes:

* five dimension scores
* formatScore

* assetType

Use one sector-level recommendation thesis and adapt only the format conditions lightly.

## Scoring Dimensions

Use these exact dimensions and weights:

* Durabilidad: 27%
* Solidez: 22%
* Adhesión: 18%
* Solvencia: 16%
* Resiliencia: 17%

Formula:

Final Score =
Durabilidad × 0.27
+ Solidez × 0.22
+ Adhesión × 0.18
+ Solvencia × 0.16
+ Resiliencia × 0.17

Round finalScore to nearest whole number.
scoreExact must include one decimal.

## Dimension Definitions

Durabilidad:
Long-term demand durability, technology resistance, secular trend, regulatory durability, and expected relevance at 10-year exit.

Solidez:
Predictability of tenant revenues, market position, competitive strength, operator quality, competitive positioning, and industry stability.

Adhesión:
Tenant stickiness, fit-out investment, relocation cost, renewal probability, space criticality, and operational dependency on location.

Solvencia:
Tenant credit quality, ability to pay rent through cycles, margin strength, guarantee quality, balance sheet quality, and default risk.

Resiliencia:
Downside protection under recession, pandemic, FX pressure, regulatory disruption, technology change, oversupply, exit liquidity, and re-tenanting risk.

## Scoring Scale

Use 0–100 per dimension.

* 85–100: exceptional tenant category
* 75–84: strong tenant category
* 65–74: acceptable / selective tenant category
* 55–64: weak or highly conditional tenant category
* 45–54: poor tenant category
* Below 45: avoid

## Tier Rules

* A+ = 85+
* A = 75–84
* B = 60–74
* C = 45–59
* D = below 45

## Cross-Dashboard Calibration — Mandatory Dimension Anchoring

You receive a full list of existing dashboard sectors with their final score and
per-dimension scores (Durabilidad, Solidez, Adhesión, Solvencia, Resiliencia).

This is not optional context. It is your scoring scale.

For every dimension score you assign to every format, you must:

1. Find the two existing sectors that should score immediately above and immediately
   below this industry on that dimension.
   - "Above" = this industry is clearly weaker on this dimension than that sector.
   - "Below" = this industry is clearly stronger on this dimension than that sector.

2. Set your score in the range between those two anchors. The upper anchor is a hard ceiling. If you score above it, you must write the
justification in the constraint field starting with "EXCEPCIÓN:" followed by
a one-sentence reason (e.g., "EXCEPCIÓN: QSR es inmune a e-commerce; ancla no
lo es"). If you cannot write that justification in one sentence, set the score
at the upper anchor level, not above it. An unjustified score above the upper
anchor is a scoring error, not a judgment call.

3. Write the two anchors in the scoringAudit:
   - positive field: "Above [Sector] ([their score])"
   - constraint field: "Below [Sector] ([their score])"

Anchoring example — new pet retail sector scoring Adhesión:
  Dashboard shows: Agro-Industrial Adh:88, Farmacéuticos Adh:85, Gimnasios Adh:65
  Pet retail has meaningful fit-out but not cold chain infrastructure.
  It is clearly below Agro-Industrial (88) and probably similar to or above Gimnasios (65).
  → Adhesión for pet retail = 67–72. Not 76. Not 80.
  positive: "Above Gimnasios (65)"
  constraint: "Below Farmacéuticos (85)"

Anchoring example — new pet retail sector scoring Solvencia:
  Dashboard shows: Supermercados Solv:77, Veterinarios Solv:35, Call Centers Solv:65
  Pet retail chains have better credit than independent vet clinics but worse than
  institutional supermarket chains.
  → Solvencia for pet retail = 55–65.
  positive: "Above Veterinarios (35)"
  constraint: "Below Supermercados (77)"

Rules:
- Never assign a dimension score without identifying both anchors.
- If you cannot identify a clear lower anchor for a dimension — meaning no existing
  sector is genuinely weaker than this industry on that dimension — write
  "Below: none identified — floor of scale" in the positive field and score
  accordingly. Do not force an anchor that isn't real.
- If an industry is genuinely exceptional on one dimension (no existing sector
  scores higher or lower), say so explicitly in the scoringAudit and justify it.
- Do not compress all scores to the middle. The anchoring is for calibration,
  not normalization. Real outliers should still score high or low.
- The anchor sectors do not have to be in the same industry. Use the most
  relevant comparison regardless of industry cluster.

## Evidence Rules

Agent 2 corrections override Agent 1.

Use this hierarchy:

1. Agent 2 Score-Eligible evidence
2. Agent 2 Needs AI Verification evidence, used conservatively
3. Agent 1 evidence not challenged by Agent 2
4. Exclude claims Agent 2 marked Exclude From Scoring

Rules:

* If Agent 2 excludes a specific claim, do not use that claim.
* Do not punish the whole industry because one data point was excluded.
* If evidence is weak but industry logic is strong, score based on logic and lower confidence.
* If CA/DR cap rates or rent benchmarks are unsupported, exclude those exact benchmarks.
* Missing cap rate or rent data should affect recommendation diligence, not automatically reduce tenant-quality score.
* If tenant credit depends on parent guarantee, do not assume the guarantee exists unless confirmed.
* If re-tenanting is difficult, reflect that in Resiliencia.
* If lease currency is uncertain, mention it in risks/recommendation; affect scores only if it changes tenant quality or default risk.
